Getting to the Venue

Firstly, to get to the venue, you MUST get to the city of Perugia. Information on getting to Perugia can be found on the "Getting There" page.
Once you get to Perugia:
Whether you are getting there by train or by plane, you can easily take a taxi from either location to the venue. Just show the driver where you intend to go and they will take you there. When Austin and I did the trip in December of 2022, it was approximately €30 for 30 minutes. They take card, but they prefer cash – they may also speak little English. Perugia and Assisi (where Villamena is), are extremely small Italian towns, there is no Uber (at least when we were there) and if there is, there are not a lot. SO take a taxi, they are reasonably priced and easy to grab at either the train station or the airport.

Concerning Children Throughout the Stay

Children are welcome to come and stay on the venue; they can join us for all the group activities – eating at the restaurants or on the villa/get togethers in various rooms – with the exception of on the day of the wedding.

  • For the day of the wedding (10.24.2024), we ask that there be no children between the ages of newborn to 10 years old at the ceremony or the reception.
    • We will be providing a babysitter service that we will on site of the villa so that the parents have access to the children at all times.
    • There will be multiple sitters if there are multiple children, especially in the case of babies and/or toddlers.
    • This service will be paid for by US so there is no need to factor this price into your budgets.
    • The service will not be all day, meaning that for the first half of the day, until approximately 3pm, the children are free to roam the villa/hotel as they wish and with their parents if needed. And it will last until the wedding festivities have ended, approximately midnight or 1am, or until all the children have been picked up.

This was a tough decision to make but we would very much appreciate it if everyone respected our wishes.
